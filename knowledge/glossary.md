# Claims Glossary

Plain-language definitions for the jargon that shows up in claim files. The agent uses these in its internal reasoning and translates them out of any customer-facing output.

**FNOL** — First Notice of Loss. The first time the insurer hears about a claim. Usually a phone call, form, or app submission.

**Reserve** — An estimate of what the insurer expects to pay out on a claim. Set by the handler, not the agent. Never communicated to the customer.

**Excess (deductible)** — The amount the customer pays toward a claim before the insurer pays the rest. Shown on the policy schedule.

**Subrogation** — The insurer's right to recover costs from a third party who caused the loss. Relevant in motor claims with an at-fault other driver.

**Indemnity** — Putting the customer back in the financial position they were in before the loss. Not betterment.

**Betterment** — When repair/replacement leaves the customer better off than before the loss (e.g., a new-for-old roof replacement on a 30-year-old roof). May reduce settlement.

**Third-party liability** — Legal responsibility the customer has to others (injury, property damage).

**Quantum** — The amount of a loss. "Liability is accepted, quantum is disputed" means we agree we're paying, we're arguing about how much.

**Reserve leakage** — Paying more on claims than necessary. A claims-operations concern, not a customer-facing concept.

**Schedule** — The document that personalizes a policy to one customer: their vehicle, address, limits, excess, named drivers, extensions.

**Extension** — An optional add-on to a base policy (e.g., Courtesy Car, Accidental Damage on a Home policy).

**Peril** — A specific cause of loss named in the policy (fire, storm, theft…).

**Exclusion** — Something the policy explicitly does not cover.

**Ex gratia payment** — A payment the insurer makes without admitting liability, usually for customer-relations reasons. Decided by the handler and their supervisor, not the agent.

**Without prejudice** — A legal phrase meaning "saying this doesn't count against me later." Used in disputed claims. The agent does not use this in customer letters unless instructed.
