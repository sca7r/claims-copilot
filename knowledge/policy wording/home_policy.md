# Synthetic Home Policy Wording — Product Code HOM-STD

**This is a synthetic policy for portfolio purposes. Not a real insurance product.**

## §1 — Who and what is insured

The policyholder, their permanent residents, and the property listed on the schedule (buildings and/or contents, as indicated).

## §2 — Buildings cover

The permanent structure of the home, including fixtures, fittings, and outbuildings within the boundary of the property, against the insured perils listed in §4.

## §3 — Contents cover

Movable household goods and personal belongings normally kept at the home. Single items worth more than €2,000 must be listed on the schedule to be covered for their full value.

## §4 — Insured perils

### §4.1 Fire, smoke, lightning, explosion
Covered.

### §4.2 Storm and flood
Covered. Storm is defined as per national meteorological data (Beaufort ≥9). Flood includes escape of water from external sources.

### §4.3 Escape of water
Leaks from plumbing, heating, or appliances are covered. Damage caused by gradual leaks that a reasonable homeowner should have noticed is excluded.

### §4.4 Theft and attempted theft
Covered, provided there is evidence of forced entry. A police report is required.

### §4.5 Accidental damage
Covered only if the Accidental Damage extension is listed on the schedule.

## §5 — Key exclusions

- Wear and tear, gradual deterioration, and maintenance issues.
- Damage from vermin, insects, or pets owned by the household.
- Damage occurring while the property has been unoccupied for more than 60 consecutive days, unless declared.
- Business use of the property, unless declared.

## §6 — Excess

The excess shown on the schedule applies per claim. A separate subsidence excess applies if subsidence cover is listed.

## §7 — Alternative accommodation

If the home becomes uninhabitable due to an insured event, we pay reasonable alternative accommodation costs up to 20% of the buildings sum insured, for up to 24 months.

## §8 — Duty of care

The policyholder must take reasonable steps to maintain the property, secure it when absent, and prevent further damage after an incident.
